## 2. Connecting Responsibilities to the "Security Pillars"
[cite_start]Every daily task serves a specific purpose within the **CIA Triad**, a framework I have prioritized throughout my 10+ years of IT experience[cite: 184].

* [cite_start]**Confidentiality:** I protect sensitive data by managing access controls and using SQL and DBMS tools to prevent unauthorized data exfiltration[cite: 186].
* [cite_start]**Integrity:** Through routine system maintenance and secure software deployment, I ensure that organizational data remains accurate and unaltered by malicious actors[cite: 187].
* [cite_start]**Availability:** I proactively troubleshoot network connectivity (LAN/WAN) and hardware failures to minimize downtime and ensure critical resources are always available[cite: 188].

## 3. The Proactive Analyst Mindset
[cite_start]A modern analyst does not simply wait for an alarm; they work to make the environment "too expensive and difficult" for an attacker to try[cite: 180, 191].

### Strategic Optimization & Hardening
* [cite_start]**Continuous Learning:** Staying updated on emerging threats and **Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)** to anticipate attacker tactics[cite: 173, 196].
* [cite_start]**Standardization:** Applying the **NIST Cybersecurity Framework** to standardize security protocols and align with global benchmarks[cite: 197].
* [cite_start]**Security-Hardening:** Leveraging my deep background in system administration to "harden" existing IT infrastructure before vulnerabilities can be exploited[cite: 198].
